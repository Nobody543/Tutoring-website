Why Me

Recency is the advantage.

Most tutors you'll come across have either just completed a degree at Oxford or Cambridge, or have been teaching for over a decade. Both sound impressive — and for A-Level preparation, they may well be excellent choices. A-Level is structured, predictable, and largely repeatable year to year.

TMUA is a different kind of challenge entirely. It doesn't repeat itself in the same way, and it's not something you can prepare for purely through content knowledge. It requires a particular way of thinking — one that is genuinely difficult to teach without having experienced the test yourself, under real conditions.

Long-established tutors, however skilled, have not sat TMUA. They never will. I have — recently, under the same conditions you'll face — and I scored 8.5. Beyond my own experience, I've spent considerable time studying the patterns behind TMUA performance: why mathematically capable candidates underperform, how errors compound under time pressure, and why some students feel well-prepared only to find the result doesn't reflect it. A recurring issue is a mismatch between the kind of thinking A-Level rewards and what TMUA actually demands — and that gap is rarely visible until it's too late. Understanding exactly where and how things go wrong is just as central to my method as knowing what gets the marks.

I've developed a structured, step-by-step method to build the specific skills TMUA demands — not generic exam technique, but a targeted approach to the mathematical reasoning and problem-solving the test is designed to assess. That's what I bring to every session.

The Plan

01
Consolidate the foundations
Ensure your A-Level knowledge is genuinely secure — not just familiar. TMUA does not test advanced content, but it demands that core material is understood with real precision. We identify and close any gaps before building on top of them.
02
Develop the problem-solving toolkit
TMUA rewards a specific kind of mathematical reasoning that goes well beyond standard A-Level technique. We build the skills of logical deduction, lateral thinking, and efficient problem-framing that the test is explicitly designed to assess.
03
Master the TMUA
Apply the method under timed, realistic conditions. We work through the full range of question types, refine your approach to time management, and address the patterns of error that most commonly cost candidates marks — including the ones that aren't obvious until it's too late.
04
Prepare for interview
Move from written test to live problem-solving. We focus on thinking out loud, responding constructively to challenge, and demonstrating the genuine mathematical curiosity that distinguishes strong interview candidates.
05
Secure the place
Arrive at every stage of the application process prepared, confident, and with a precise understanding of what you're being assessed on. The whole programme is built around this outcome from the very first session.
